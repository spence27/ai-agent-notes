What it does to agents

Most agents are invisible to it, or described wrong.

01

Invisible

Ask ChatGPT for the "best agent in [a town]" and the local top producer often simply isn't in the answer. Someone else is.

02

Misrepresented

When AI does name an agent, it can get the story wrong — an old brokerage, stale numbers. There's a live example below.

03

Unmonitored

Nobody's checking. Nothing in a normal agent's stack surfaces any of this — it all happens out of view.

What it is, under the hood

Being #1 on Google doesn't mean an AI knows you exist.

What it is

Getting ChatGPT, Google's AI, and the rest to find an agent, get them right, and recommend them when a buyer asks. All of it rides on signals the AI already trusts. None of it is a hidden trick.

What it isn't

A setting to flip, a file to upload, or classic SEO under a new name. And it isn't a single number — which is a big part of why it's so easy to fake.

The part that surprised me most

There is no single "AI visibility." The engines flatly disagree.

One question, "best real estate agents in Longmont," with no names in it. I ran it 7–10 times on each engine, for two real Colorado agents. Here's what came back.

Messier than "invisible"

The results are unstable, and AI invents facts.

Unstable

Ask twice, get two answers

Run the same question back-to-back and the names change. The honest number is a rate — how many runs out of ten an agent shows up in. A "report" built on one screenshot is mostly noise.

Wrong

It makes up your story

In 9 of my 10 ChatGPT runs, it pinned one of those agents to a brokerage she'd already left. Perplexity got it wrong too, with a different wrong answer. AI states things about people that simply aren't true.

What actually works

The obvious levers, and the less obvious ones.

The first layer is table stakes — every vendor lists it:

01
Reviews — volume and recency; still the biggest signal AI reads.
02
Google Business Profile, which is what Google's AI leans on most.
03
The "best agent" lists (Expertise, U.S. News, etc.), where ChatGPT reads from.
04
One consistent identity across the web, so AI knows it's all one person.
The second layer is less obvious — and almost nobody is doing it
1

The numbers don't sit still

Ask again a month later and the names have moved. The engines update, the sources shift, and an agent who showed up half the time can quietly stop showing up. Any one-time "AI audit" is a photo of the weather.

2

Wrong facts don't fix themselves

That wrong-brokerage answer isn't a glitch. The mistake lives in the sources the AI reads, so it comes back every time someone asks — and the machine repeats it with total confidence until the source itself gets corrected.

3

The answers come from somewhere

A lot of what AI repeats about local agents traces back to a handful of Reddit and forum threads — real people asking "who's good in [town]?" Those threads sit upstream of the answers. (The shortcut version, paying to seed fake ones, gets accounts banned and doesn't survive the next model update.)

4

A lot of agent sites are blank to AI

Blocked crawlers, or everything rendered in JavaScript so the machine sees an empty page. That part is real and fixable. The "llms.txt" and "AI schema" files being sold everywhere, meanwhile, get ignored by the very engines they claim to influence.

The catch worth flagging

This space is already full of confident nonsense.

A cottage industry has formed around selling agents "AI optimization." Plenty of what it sells does nothing.

llms.txt files "GEO schema" packages mass AI city pages paid Reddit placement "guaranteed ChatGPT placement" "first AI calls in 6 weeks"

And from the outside, an agent has almost no way to tell the real thing from the placebo.

The short version
Where I've landed, for now: the buyer shift is real, and it can actually be measured — that's the part I didn't expect. The industry forming on top of it mostly sells certainty it doesn't have. Still pulling on this.
